Henry David Thoreau

“I find it wholesome to be alone the greater part of the time. To be in company, even with the best, is soon wearisome and dissipating. I love to be alone. I never found the companion that was so companionable as solitude.” - Walden (1854)


A brief timeline of his life:

  • 1817 Born on July 12 in Concord, Massachusetts
  • 1837 Meets Ralph Waldo Emerson after Emerson's "The American Scholar" lecture, setting him on the path of the Transcendentalists
  • 1845 Begins his two-year stay at Walden Pond
  • 1848 Spends the night in jail for refusing to pay the poll tax, protesting slavery and the war with Mexico
  • 1849 Publishes essay "Civil Disobedience"
  • 1849 Publishes A Week on the Concord and Merrimack Rivers, which fails to sell
  • 1854 Publishes Walden, which is well-received
  • 1862 Dies on May 6 after a long battle with tuberculosis

Many people pronounce his last name with the accent on the second syllable (ther-O), perhaps because of the triphthong "eau", but the proper pronunciation is like that of the word "thorough" (THER-o or THOR-o). Good luck fighting that battle, though!
